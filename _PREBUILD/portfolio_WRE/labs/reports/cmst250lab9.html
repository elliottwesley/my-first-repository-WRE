Objectives
The purpose of this lab was to introduce video editing software that we will be using to develop our final projects: a research project video.  To learn to use this software, we were instructed to make something much simpler: a brief 10-20 second video illustrating a film method known as the "Hitch cut".  This lab comprised two exercises: the first was video downloading and editing, the second video publishing.

Laboratory computer (we used Macintosh computers)

Google Chrome (internet browser)
Adobe Premier Pro (video editing software)

Notes, Observations, and Questions
DISCLAIMER: The purpose of this project was to learn how video editing software can be used to combine clips into a video.  As this was a teaching exercise, the use of the procured clips in this project constitutes fair use according to Section 107 of the US Copyright Act (Chapter 1: Subject Matter and Scope of Copyright).
Exercise 1 of 2: Video downloading and editing
In this day and age, many applets and software products exist for the purpose of downloading videos from the internet and saving them as files to be used in projects such as this one.  Such applications come and go, and for this assignment we used a bookmarklet provided by deturl.com, whose purpose is downloading internet videos for fair rights use.  It is not meant to be used as an alternative to torrenting software (which is controversial as it is because of the portion of people who use said software to pirate movies and television shows).
Using this bookmarklet, we were instructed to download a video from YouTube embarrasingly titled Hitchcock Loves Bikinis.  In this video, legendary filmmaker Alfred Hitchcock explains how clips can be put together in a film to convey an idea--in other words, the effect we sought to replicate in this exercise.  From this clip we would use video editing software (such as Adobe Premier Pro) to cut out the example of the Hitch Cut in the original video (the character, represented by Alfred Hitchcock, a short clip of the woman and her child, and finally another shot of Alfred Hitchcock smiling in reaction to the clip before).  The second of these two clips had to be removed, so I put two copies of the full clip in and changed the endpoints to only show what I needed.
Then, we were to use the same deturl bookmarklet to find any other clip on YouTube which we would use to replace the second clip in the example.  I decided to use the screaming goat meme (or screaming sheep, as the title of this clip says).  Then, like with the snip from the Hitchcock video, I inserted the goat clip where the woman-and-child shot used to be (Fig. 1).  After tweaking the length of the goat clip, I exported the project as a .mov file.
