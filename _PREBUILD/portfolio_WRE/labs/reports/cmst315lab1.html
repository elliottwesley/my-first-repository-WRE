Notes, Observations, and Questions
Our original plan was to work individually at first, with the idea that we each would come up with our separate ideas and then converge to discuss. I thought of a trick that relates to the binary counting scheme.  Knowing that binary is a base 2 counting scheme, I thought providing a table of the outcomes of the expression 2^n up to the amount of nodes (not hosts) initially given in the scenario (which was 32,768) would help in determining a method [Figure 1].  From this list, the next step of my idea was to determine the lowest doubles of two that still satisfy the needs of our clients, beginning with the largest of the four because of the nature of VLSM.  
Figure 2, shows a graphic of the distribution we agreed on and presented, which was generated based on the above method.
